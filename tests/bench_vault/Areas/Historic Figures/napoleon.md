# Napoleon Bonaparte

> [!abstract] Why he matters here
> Napoleon is a case study in work ethic and decisiveness — preparation
> compounded into what looked, from the outside, like genius.

## Work ethic

Napoleon worked eighteen-hour days and slept in fragments. He dictated to
multiple secretaries at once, switching subjects without losing the thread. His
advantage was rarely raw intelligence; it was volume of organized effort. He
read constantly, planned campaigns to the last supply wagon, and rehearsed
contingencies so that on the day a decision felt instant.

## Decisiveness

"Take time to deliberate, but when the time for action has arrived, stop
thinking and go in." He gathered information ruthlessly, then committed. Most
people lose not to a better plan but to their own hesitation.

## Discipline and timing

He mastered the art of timing — striking when an enemy was divided, refusing
battle when the odds were wrong. Discipline, for him, was not grim restraint but
the freedom that preparation buys: when you have done the work, boldness is
cheap.

## Lessons

- Out-prepare everyone; let it look like talent.
- Decide fast once the facts are in; hesitation is the real enemy.
- Control the timing of the fight whenever you can.
