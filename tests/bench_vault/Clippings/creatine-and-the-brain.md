# Creatine and the Brain

> [!abstract] Summary
> Creatine buffers ATP in the brain, not just the muscle. The standard, well
> studied dose is 5 g per day; emerging research looks at higher doses for
> cognition under stress.

## What it does

Creatine recycles ATP, the cell's energy currency. The brain is metabolically
expensive, so a larger phosphocreatine reserve can help under load — sleep
deprivation, intense cognitive work, or aging.

## Dose

The maintenance dose for muscle is about 5 g of creatine monohydrate per day; a
loading phase is optional. For cognitive benefit the literature is younger, and
some studies use higher doses (up to ~10 g), but 5 g daily is the safe,
evidence-backed baseline. It is one of the most studied supplements in existence,
with a strong safety record in healthy adults.

## Caveats

Effects on cognition are clearest when the brain is stressed (e.g. sleep
deprived). Well-rested, well-fed people may notice less. Hydrate; the only common
side effect is minor water retention early on.
