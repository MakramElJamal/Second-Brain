# The 48 Laws of Power

> [!abstract] Summary
> Robert Greene's field guide to power: how it is gained, kept, and lost. Read
> defensively as much as offensively.

## Laws worth remembering

- **Law 1 — Never outshine the master.** Make those above you feel superior.
- **Law 3 — Conceal your intentions.** Keep people off balance; never reveal the
  purpose behind your actions until it is too late to stop you.
- **Law 4 — Always say less than necessary.** Silence reads as power.
- **Law 5 — Guard your reputation with your life.** Reputation is the cornerstone
  of power.

## How to read it

Greene is descriptive, not prescriptive — a map of how power actually moves, so
you are neither naive nor needlessly cynical. The most useful laws are the
defensive ones: spotting these moves when they are used on you.
